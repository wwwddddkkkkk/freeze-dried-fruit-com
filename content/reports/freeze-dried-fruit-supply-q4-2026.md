## Q4 snapshot {#snapshot}

Q4 2026 is the year's transition quarter. U.S. cranberry and California pomegranate harvest, the Mexican Keitt window finishes, Brazilian Palmer mango hits peak volume, and Chilean blueberry begins its long ramp into Q1 2027. Holiday demand for retail snack pouches and gift packs is at its annual peak.

For buyers, Q4 is a two-track conversation. The first track is fulfilling current-year holiday demand — snack pouches, gift packs, and ingredient blends moving through retail and foodservice channels. The second track is locking in Q1 2027 supply: Chilean blueberry, Peruvian aguaymanto and lúcuma, and the rest of the Southern Hemisphere program. Buyers who try to negotiate Q1 contracts in January typically pay more than buyers who closed them in October–November.

## 1. North America — cranberry, pomegranate, late mango {#north-america}

### U.S. cranberry

Cranberry harvest runs September–November, concentrated in Massachusetts, Wisconsin, New Jersey, and Oregon. The freeze-dried cranberry supply pool is small relative to fresh and dried cranberry, and most premium freeze-dried cranberry is pre-contracted with established buyers. Buyers wanting freeze-dried cranberry for new Q4/Q1 product launches should be talking to suppliers now — the available capacity gets booked by mid-Q4.

### California pomegranate

Pomegranate runs September–December, with peak in October–November. Freeze-dried pomegranate arils are a premium product with relatively small supply. The format complexity (separating arils from membrane, maintaining ruby color, controlling breakage) limits the number of processors. Premium pomegranate aril supply for Q4 holiday pouches and Q1 2027 ingredient programs should be locked in now.

### Mexican Keitt finishing

The Keitt window tails through November. Mexican processors begin transitioning their lines to alternative inputs for Q1 — Tommy Atkins year-round, late Peruvian (for processors with Andean supply lines), or Brazilian Palmer (for export channels).

### Brazilian Palmer peak

Palmer hits peak volume October–November. For U.S. and EU buyers wanting alternate-season mango supply that isn't dependent on Indian or Mexican origins, Palmer is the Q4 staple.

### Australian R2E2

R2E2 opens in November. Australian freeze-dried mango is a relatively small export category, but it provides Q4/Q1 alternate-hemisphere supply for buyers diversifying away from Asian origins.

## 2. Southern Hemisphere bridge {#southern-hemisphere-bridge}

### Chilean blueberry begins

Chilean blueberry harvest begins in November in the Bío-Bío and Maule regions, ramping into peak December–February. Chilean freeze-drying operations are gearing up. Q4 is when buyers should be:

- Confirming Q1 2027 volume contracts with Chilean processors
- Discussing variety mix (high-bush versus low-bush, organic versus conventional)
- Locking in pricing at season-discount levels rather than waiting for Q1 spot

### Peruvian aguaymanto, lúcuma, mango

Peruvian fruit programs run year-round, but Q4 is when Peruvian processors negotiate Q1 export commitments. Mexican-style Ataulfo from Peruvian growers (the December–March window) competes with Mexican supply at the start of Q1.

### Maqui pre-season

Chilean maqui wild-harvest doesn't open until January, but Q4 is the planning quarter for the upcoming wild-harvest season. Premium maqui buyers should be in conversation with their Mapuche-community-partnered processors about expected Q1 yields.

## 3. Holiday demand surge {#demand-surge}

Q4 is the annual peak for retail snack pouch demand. The dynamics:

- **Gift packs and seasonal multi-packs** drive snack-grade fruit demand
- **Cereal, granola, and bar formulators** stock ingredient inventory for Q1 production
- **Foodservice and meal-kit programs** lock in Q4/Q1 ingredient supply
- **Subscription box and DTC brands** ramp Q4 ordering for January launches

For freeze-drying operations, this creates capacity tightness through November. Buyers who didn't lock in Q4 production slots by mid-Q3 are competing for limited spot availability.

### Cranberry-pomegranate seasonal positioning

Q4 is also when seasonal freeze-dried products built around cranberry, pomegranate, apple, and citrus reach retail. Brands launching new holiday SKUs typically need production complete by mid-October to hit Black Friday retail dates.

## 4. Pricing & freight signals {#pricing}

**Cranberry and pomegranate pricing** in Q4 reflects the small, contracted nature of the supply pool. Spot-market availability is limited; pricing premium reflects both scarcity and the premium positioning of the finished products.

**Mango pricing** in Q4 sees commodity-tier supply (Tommy Atkins, Palmer) at lower prices as the premium Indian/Pakistani volumes finished long ago.

**Berry pricing** softens as Chilean Q1 supply begins to take the bridge role from ending Northern Hemisphere inventory. Buyers contracting forward at Q4 prices for Q1 supply typically save 5–15% versus Q1 spot.

**Freight rates** see Q4 surge from holiday and post-holiday volume. Late Q4 ocean freight from Asia to North America and EU typically sees 10–20% rate increases versus Q3 baseline, with Lunar New Year pressure adding more volatility from late January.

**Currency** matters more in Q4 as buyers begin pricing Q1 2027 contracts in foreign currency. CLP, PEN, and MXN exposure all need to be considered.

## 5. Q1 2027 outlook {#2027-outlook}

Buyers should already be:

- **Closing Chilean blueberry contracts** for Q1 2027 supply at Q4 (peak season) pricing
- **Confirming Peruvian aguaymanto and lúcuma** volumes for the new year
- **Discussing maqui** wild-harvest volume expectations with Mapuche-community processors
- **Planning Indian Alphonso pre-season** — the April 2027 window starts being negotiated in Q4 2026
- **Reviewing FSMA/FSVP documentation** for cross-border supplier verification before Q1 imports

The strongest Q4 buyers treat this quarter as half operations, half planning. The buyers who get to mid-January with no Q1 supply commitments in place are usually the same buyers who paid premium pricing through 2026.

---

Q4 2026 closes a strong supply year and sets up Q1 2027. The freeze-dried fruit category continues to mature, and the gap between disciplined sourcing operations and reactive ones widens further every quarter. The 2027 winners will mostly come from the disciplined group.
