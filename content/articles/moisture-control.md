Freeze-dried fruit is highly sensitive to moisture. Once exposed to humid air, the fruit can begin absorbing water and lose its crisp texture. This is why packaging matters so much. Barrier films, seals, desiccants, oxygen absorbers, and pouch size can all affect how long the product stays crunchy.

## What "low moisture" actually means

Most premium freeze-dried fruit targets 1–4% residual moisture. At those levels the fruit feels dry, snaps cleanly, and stores well. Even small increases — a few percent — show up as softer, leathery pieces.

> The crunch you feel is essentially a moisture spec. Lose the spec, lose the crunch.

## Where moisture sneaks in

- A bag seal that wasn't fully closed at the factory
- A pouch with thin barrier film that lets humidity creep in
- Headspace too large, allowing moist air with each open
- Storage in a humid kitchen or warm shelf
- Resealing too slowly after each serving

## What buyers can ask suppliers

Ask for the target moisture and water activity, the packaging film spec, the desiccant or oxygen absorber strategy, and shelf-life testing data. The answer tells you whether the supplier treats moisture as a casual concern or a controlled spec.
